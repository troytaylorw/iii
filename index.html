INTERSECTIONAL INTERFACE INDEX
This is a database of current designers and artists who are rethinking the interface and digital space. The Intersectional Interface Index takes inspiration from Polymode’s Queer History Timeline, Mindy Seu’s Cyberfeminism Index, and Working Not Working’s Where Are The Black Designers. By focusing on web designers and online practitioners, the hope for this space is to act as a living group show and community of radical digital thinkers.

If you'd like to contribute to the Intersectional Interface Index, please visit the Are.na channel below and add a link to a person or project who might find kin in this database. Please keep in mind that this site intends to center people of color, particularly queer, femme voices. Submissions will be collected and added on an ongoing basis.
Legacy Russell
Legacy Russell is a curator, writer, and artist whose creative work focuses on gender, performance, digital selfdom, internet idolatry, and new media ritual. Her book, Glitch Feminism is a vital new chapter in cyberfeminism, one that explores the relationship between gender, technology and identity.

American Artist
AMERICAN ARTIST makes thought experiments that mine the history of technology, race, and knowledge production, beginning with their legal name change in 2013. Their artwork primarily takes the form of sculpture, software, and single-channel video. Artist is a 2021 Regents’ Lecturer at UCLA and teaches critical theory at the School for Poetic Computation.

Their essay "Black Gooey Universe" outlines the history of the graphic user interface, and Silicon Valley's relationship with Blackness.

Yehwan Song
Yehwan Song is an artist and designer questioning standardized design and interface conventions that frame users' behavior and the templates that make them lose their content awareness and become accustomed to oversimplification. She constructs outside-the-frame devices and interfaces in order to challenge the notion of user-friendliness. She pursues diversity instead of consistency and respects variety in the web environment above efficiency.

Somnath Bhatt
Artist Somnath Bhatt’s work tempers the personal with macro-social issues faced by the ever-changing world today. His body of enigmatic works brings our full attention to the doctrine of individuality and pushes us to consider the ways in which we can shift our gaze for good. While Bhatt now lives in the US, he was raised in Ahmedabad so his protean work, mostly made of digital illustration, visual images and precise algorithms are shaped by the local makers, stories, craft traditions and long-standing rituals.

Olivia McKayla Ross
Olivia McKayla Ross is a Caribbean American video artist, programmer, and poet from Queens, New York City. Her work is inspired by the relationship between electronic video and vanity--by deep fantasy, Instagram filters, glamour magic, mirrors, and the fantasies and anxieties of video transmission: immersion, absorption, surveillance, and control.

She is currently Associate Producer of the Black Feminist speculative documentary Seeking Mavis Beacon, while pursuing a dual degree in Design and Technology and Screen Studies at The New School. Olivia is the youngest alum of the School for Poetic Computation and has taught at Black Girls Code, BUFU, POWRPLNT, Ethel’s Club, Anderson Ranch, and Pioneer Works.

Ethel's Club
A Black-owned social and wellness club designed to celebrate people of color, online and IRL.

Ethel's Club is the first social and wellness platform designed to celebrate people of color in both physical and digital spaces. Our vision is to spark the next renaissance with people of color at the forefront.

At Ethel's Club, we see our members as unique individuals that deserve care, celebration, and healing. We believe that when we’re given the tools to thrive as individuals, we create new worlds when we come together as a community. We’re dedicated to providing those tools to encourage healing, inspiration, and communal care.

DESIGN TO DIVEST
We are a Black-led collective of designers, artists, technologists and strategists designing equitable futures by divesting from inequitable institutions.
‍
By Equity we mean fairness and ownership. We must create new institutions and new systems where Black and marginalized people have true equity in our communities and society at large.
‍
By Divestment we mean reducing (until elimination) systems of eurocentrism, white supremacy, patriarchy, dehumanization and oppression wherever they are found.

Somewhere Good
"A social playground made for us. Somewhere Good is a social platform designed for people of color to connect around the things we love. A place online where every click is a reflection of you.

As a group of Black, Latinx and queer people, we are in constant pursuit of online experiences that reflect what we love and who we are. Throughout our lives, it’s been incredibly difficult to find safe online (and in-person) spaces. Somewhere Good will offer the tools, relationships and opportunities to design and actualize the futures we envision."
